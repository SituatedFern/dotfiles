The Poetics:

The ancient greeks were legendary for their ideas on philosophy and politics. Stocism and Skeptisism were created by the greeks as ways of interperating the world around them. Although these ideas were formed a long time ago, they are still relevant and applicable to our lives today. Many of the famous philosophers of ancient greece also analyzed and commented on the work of other people. This includes artistic pieces and even other philosophers' beliefs. Aristotle was a prominent figure in this field of study. He often commented on plays that were being shown at the time. In fact, Aristotle had a huge role in defining what a tragedy actually is. Aristotle went on to write a book about comedies and tragedies as well, called The Poetics.
